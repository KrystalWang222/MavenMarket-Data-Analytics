Project Background

  • Business context: (e.g.) Multi‑store retail performance tracking (1997–1998), focusing on targets vs actuals and cost structure.
  • Data source: Transactions, returns, products, stores/regions, customers, and a date table.
  • Audience: Business leaders · FP&A · Operations.
  • Core questions: Revenue vs target, gross margin %, operating expenses, contribution by division/category/store, returns & net sales.

Approach

  • KPIs defined: Total Revenue, Net Sales, Gross Margin %, EBIT%, rolling revenue, returns ratio.
  • Star schema: Calendar/Products/Stores/Regions/Customers dimensions related to Transaction/Return facts.
  • DAX metrics: rolling windows (DATESINPERIOD), period‑over‑period (DATEADD), target vs actual (e.g., Revenue Target = prior month × 1.05).
  • Visual flow: topline overview → drilldowns to division/category/store → regional and SKU contribution.

Data Cleaning & Methods

  • Data quality Checks: null order IDs, negative invoice values split as Sales Credit, zero cost/qty sanity checks.
  • Indirect costs: consistent allocation (Admin/MoH/Distribution) for cross‑department comparison.
  • Time intelligence: rolling 60‑day revenue, last‑month comparisons, YoY/MoM deltas.
  • Targeting: gauge vs target to monitor trajectory and alert on underperformance.

Key Findings (paired with the report)

  • Revenue vs Budget: Outperformed budget and prior year; momentum led by Healthcare and Distributor channels.
  • Margins & Costs: COGS up YoY yet margin steady (~64%); focus on discount‑heavy SKUs.
  • OpEx pressure: Admin/MoH overruns drive weaker EBIT; scenario‑plan 5–10% OpEx reductions.
  • Regional/Store dispersion: NE shows the largest improvement; top stores drive disproportionate net sales.

Takeaways / Recommendations

  • Run cost‑cut scenarios (5–10%) prioritizing Admin/MoH; monitor EBIT recovery path.
  • Double down on high‑margin categories and top‑performing regions/stores; trim low‑ROI marketing.
  • Build a returns quality dashboard to target high‑return SKUs and training opportunities.
